REVISED Q3 — GEOGRAPHIC DATA SOURCE: OFFLINE-TOLERANT GEOCODING
================================================================================

The project owner's requirement is that a missing internet connection must
never interrupt the user's workflow. The app must handle the offline case
silently and resolve it in the background when connectivity is restored.

DESIGN DIRECTION:

  CITY ENTRY (user-facing):
    When the user enters a new city during trip logging, the app searches
    the local city database first. If the city is not found locally and no
    internet is available, the app creates the city record immediately with
    the name and a geocode_status of PENDING — and lets the user continue
    uninterrupted. No error, no block, no forced wait.

  GEOCODING QUEUE:
    Any city record with geocode_status = PENDING is queued for resolution.
    When internet connectivity is detected (on app start, on network change,
    or on a periodic background check), the app processes the queue:
      - Calls Nominatim for each pending city
      - On success: populates latitude, longitude, sets geocode_status = RESOLVED
      - On failure: retries next time; failure does not surface to the user
        unless it persists across multiple sessions

  DATA MODEL IMPLICATION — cities table must include:
    - geocode_status: enum PENDING | RESOLVED
    - latitude: nullable (null until resolved)
    - longitude: nullable (null until resolved)
    - geocode_attempted_at: timestamp of last attempt (for retry logic)

  FRONTEND BEHAVIOUR for PENDING cities:
    - The city appears normally in all trip and item views — it is fully usable
    - No map pin is rendered for PENDING cities (coordinates unknown)
    - If the map view is open when coordinates resolve, the pin should appear
      without requiring a manual refresh (FRONTEND to handle gracefully)
    - No error state is shown to the user; PENDING is a silent background state

  WHEN INTERNET IS AVAILABLE AND CITY IS ALREADY IN LOCAL DB:
    No geocoding call is made. City coordinates are already stored. Fast path.

  WHEN INTERNET IS AVAILABLE AND CITY IS NEW:
    Nominatim is called immediately. City is created with geocode_status =
    RESOLVED and coordinates populated. No queue entry needed.

  WHEN INTERNET IS NOT AVAILABLE AND CITY IS NEW:
    City is created immediately with geocode_status = PENDING. User continues.
    Queue entry created. Resolved silently on next connectivity.

IMPACT ON ARCHITECTURE DECISIONS LOG

Add to your architecture decisions log:

  DECISION: Offline-tolerant city geocoding via queue-and-resolve pattern
  RATIONALE: Project owner requirement that absent internet must never block
    the user's workflow. City records are created immediately regardless of
    connectivity; coordinates are enriched asynchronously.
  IMPLICATION: BACKEND needs a geocoding service component with connectivity
    detection and retry logic. FRONTEND must render gracefully for cities
    with null coordinates (no map pin, no error).
  FUTURE: If the app is ever migrated to a hosted environment, the geocoding
    queue becomes a background job — the same pattern, different runtime.
