BACKGROUND
PO testing on 2026-03-09 identified that the current country shading logic (shade
country on any city visit) is incorrect. The PO specified the correct rule:

  Country shading should apply ONLY when:
    (a) region tier is disabled
    (b) city has no region assigned
    (c) all regions in the country have been visited

The current spec §4 colours a country on any city visit — this must be amended.

---

OPEN QUESTION — COO RESOLUTION

The PO asked: "In case (c), does 'all regions visited' mean at least one city per
region on a completed trip?"

COO interpretation (no PO clarification received in 48h; unblocking now):

  A region is "visited" when any trip_place exists whose city belongs to that
  region, regardless of trip status (planning, in_progress, or completed).

Rationale: all other shading logic treats any trip_place as evidence of intent
to visit or actual visit. Restricting country-level roll-up to completed trips
only would create a confusing asymmetry (regions shade while planning; the country
would not roll up until the trip is marked complete). The permissive interpretation
is consistent with the existing model.

Document this interpretation in the amended spec. If the PO later provides a
different answer, the change is isolated to the SQL condition — no structural work.
