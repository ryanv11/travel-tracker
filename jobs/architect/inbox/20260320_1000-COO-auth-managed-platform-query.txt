PO has reviewed your auth recommendations. One revision to discuss.

CONTEXT

Your recommendation was jose + roll-your-own OAuth PKCE. Part of the rationale
framed this as a personal/family app where Google dependency is an acceptable risk.

The PO has flagged that "family app" framing should not drive architectural
decisions. The ambition is broader distribution and real scale. At 10k MAU the
team can afford to pay for a managed auth platform — that is not a blocker.

PO DIRECTION

The PO has decided on Clerk as the managed auth platform. This is a consumer
app — no B2B, no SAML, no enterprise identity. Clerk is the correct fit.
WorkOS was considered and ruled out (enterprise-first, features irrelevant to
this use case).

Rationale for Clerk over roll-your-own:
- Simplicity: OAuth providers, token handling, refresh logic, MFA, magic links
  are all handled. We don't own the security-critical maintenance surface.
- Early scale: free tier covers early growth; pricing at real MAU is a good
  problem to have.
- Provider flexibility from day one: adding Apple, GitHub, or email+password
  is a config toggle, not a code change.

QUESTION TO ARCHITECT

Is there a technical reason to avoid Clerk specifically for this stack?

Specifically:
- Any compatibility concern with the Express backend or our JWT/req.user pattern?
- Any concern with the Electron or future iOS (Capacitor) client flows?
- Any lock-in risk that would be hard to unwind if we later need to migrate?
- Anything about our schema design (users table, refresh_tokens) that changes
  under Clerk — do we still own a users table, or does Clerk become the source
  of truth for user identity?

If there is no blocker, proceed on the assumption that Clerk is confirmed and
provide implementation guidance: how Clerk integrates with our Express backend,
what the req.user pattern looks like, and how the Electron/iOS flows are handled.
